Rescue Riders

Non-Profit Redesign

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Connecting Pets to Forever Homes

PROBLEM

The Rescue Riders’ current website lacks a straightforward navigation system, does not adequately communicate their passion for connecting pets to their forever home, and fails to convey reliability or trustworthiness. This deters potential users from taking advantage of transportation services, limiting their adoption range and denying many pets a second-chance at finding a forever home.

SOLUTION

How might we reimagine the Rescue Riders’ website so that future-pet owners experience enhanced and intuitive navigation that builds confidence and comfort in using Rescue Rider’s services -- ultimately leading to more adoptions?

USER INTERVIEWS:

What Do Users Need From Their Pet Transport Service?

We set out to assess what individuals need and expect in the process of booking a transportation service for a newly adopted pet.

Our research goals were to: 1. Uncover which factors influence individuals’ decisions to adopt a pet. 2. Understand how individuals typically go about the process of adopting a pet. 3. Understand if individuals have experience with or knowledge of pet transportation services when adopting pets from across the country.

RESEARCH INSIGHTS:

Building Trust Is Our Priority

3 out of 5 users expressed concerns about trustworthiness when arranging the adoption and transportation of an animal online.

Users reported credibility, consistent communication, and value alignment as their primary needs in choosing a transport service.

Reviews, word-of-mouth, and the quality of a website revealed to be most influential in crafting users’ trust in a pet transportation service.

USABILITY TEST SYNTHESIS:

Users Crave Clarity.

We conducted five usability tests of current website’s navigation and information hierarchy to evaluate how users’ experiences shape their attitudes towards Rescue Riders. We found:

Users need a better understanding of the transportation process.

The current website implements PDF’s where there could be web pages instead.

A map would help users visually break down routes, pick-up locations, and drop-off locations.
